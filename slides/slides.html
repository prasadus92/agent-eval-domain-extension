Executive summary 02 / 12

Food Delivery v1 in 16 weeks, ~3,200 RL-ready trajectories; the same infrastructure ships domain 2 in 8 weeks.

Situation Published tau-bench-extension programs already cover domains like Manufacturing, with E-commerce, Hotel, Insurance, Digital Wallet, and Consulting in progress across the field. This design targets Food Delivery as the first of several new domains for a frontier lab consuming the data. The brief points to v1 of tau-bench, which the repo's own README flags as deprecated and redirects to τ³-bench. We extend τ³-bench (current).
Complication The brief asks for RL training data. Most published tau-bench extensions, including the published Manufacturing release, are eval-shaped: ~50 hand-crafted tasks scored 0/1 by strict DB-hash equality. RL-shaped data needs verifier-based dense reward and broader bucket coverage; AReaL-SEA showed the payoff, lifting Pass^1 from baseline ~50% to 75% (retail) and 98% (telecom) on tau²-bench-style tasks via per-instance verifiers. Eight retail and airline assumptions break in food delivery (multi-party, real-time, stochastic, allergen safety, two-sided marketplace, monetary identity). Four reward-shape failures follow from those breaks.
Answer Three moves deliver v1 and the playbook: (1) plug a τ³-shaped Food Delivery domain into Sierra's architecture; (2) layer four targeted reward upgrades that turn binary outcomes into dense RL signal; (3) extract reusable infrastructure so domain 2 lands in 8 weeks. 12 capability buckets, weekly slices from week 4, 4 FTE core plus tiered contractor pyramid (~85 active at peak), 50% of v1 infra reused on domain 2 and rising to ~65% by domain 3.
Sources: github.com/sierra-research/tau2-bench, arXiv:2406.12045, arXiv:2506.07982, 02 / 12
What we extend, not invent 03 / 12

We build on two existing chasses, Sierra's τ³-bench architecture and the published RL-gym methodology, and position Food Delivery within the broader agentic benchmark landscape.

Sierra τ³-bench (the architecture)

Brief points to v1 (sierra-research/tau-bench); v1's README redirects to τ³-bench (sierra-research/tau2-bench).

  • Domain contract. Four Python files plus four data files plug a new domain into the eval and RL framework. Submittable to taubench.com.
  • Gymnasium RL interface via uv sync --extra gym. Drops directly into RL training pipelines.
  • Compositional task generator from τ²-bench. Verifiable task synthesis at scale via atomic action primitives.
  • Dual-control (Dec-POMDP). Both agent and user have tools. Food delivery has 4 actors; we extend to multi-actor.
  • 75+ task fixes from the SABER paper (Cuadron et al., ICLR 2026).

RL-gym methodology (from public sources)

Public framing on and the tau-bench-extension blog. We adopt the same vocabulary.

  • ~50% pass-rate calibration target for "Tau-style RL-gyms." We hit it via difficulty stratification.
  • Three-strategy QA: human-in-the-loop review, multi-layered validation, collaborative evolution.
  • "Can your AI say no?" framing for policy-aware agents. Bucket 7 is built around it.
  • Failure taxonomy: tool execution, data grounding, reasoning. Drives our auto-error-identification.
  • Existing program slot. Manufacturing live; E-commerce, Hotel, Insurance, Digital Wallet, Consulting in development. Food Delivery slots in, does not replace.
Where Food Delivery sits in the landscape. SWE-bench Verified (500 tasks, top frontier model ~93% Pass^1) tests coding agents. AgentBench (8 environments, 29 LLMs) tests general capability. Mind2Web 2 (130 tasks, 1,000+ human-hours) and WebArena (812 tasks) test browser agents. GAIA (466 tasks) tests assistant-style reasoning. OSWorld (369 tasks, humans 72% vs best model 12%) tests OS agents. Tau-bench's lane is multi-turn tool use under domain policy with a simulated user. Food Delivery sits in that lane and adds marketplace primitives the others do not exercise.

What this means for our build

Typed actions, in-place mutation. Tools mutate data dict in place; that mutation is what reward hashes after the trajectory ends. Our Food Delivery domain ships 24 agent tools and 12 user tools (3 personas × 4 each) following the same ABC.
Reward = strict hash equality. SHA-256 of the agent's final DB state must equal the hash after replaying ground-truth actions. Strict 0/1, no partial credit, no tolerance for stochastic outcomes, no safety constraints. Fine for eval, wrong shape for RL training.
Where our four reward upgrades attach. We extend calculate_reward() with: (1) canonical hashing on equivalence-class fields (handles stochastic driver assignment); (2) safety-gate predicate (hard-zeros allergen violations); (3) monetary-identity check (accepts any valid refund / credit / replacement combination summing to the disputed amount); (4) per-checkpoint process reward (dense RL signal).
Backwards-compatible. Tasks without reward_overrides reduce to base τ³ reward. Existing retail and airline leaderboards still work; food delivery and future domains layer their overrides per task.

The differentiator 08 / 12

Base τ³ reward is binary 0/1, fine for eval, wrong for RL. Four targeted upgrades close the failure modes food delivery surfaces.

1 · equivalence-class hashing
Stochastic outcomes don't break reward.

Driver assignment is non-deterministic; any driver in the valid candidate pool is correct. Canonical hashing on candidate-set projection succeeds where strict equality fails. ~10 LOC, buckets 5, 6, 10.

2 · safety-gate hard rules
Allergen violation hard-zeros the episode.

Some violations cannot be recovered. Pre-check predicate runs against the trajectory; if violated, reward = 0 unconditionally. ~30 LOC + DSL, buckets 7, 9, 12.

3 · monetary-identity invariant
refunds + credits + replacement = disputed

A refund scenario has many valid resolutions. Reward checks the identity, not one specific ledger pattern. With hard_zero_if_above, "Can your AI say no?" becomes trainable in one signal. ~25 LOC, buckets 3, 7, 8, 11.

4 · dense process reward
Per-checkpoint partial credit unlocks RL.

Sparse 0/1 is hard credit-assignment. Each task ships ~5 checkpoint predicates with weights. The lab trains on outcome, process, or any custom blend. ~50 LOC + DSL, 100× signal density.

Upgrade 2 in code, the allergen safety gate

tau2/domains/food_delivery/reward.py (excerpt)def _safety_gate_passed(self) -> bool:
    gates = self.task.reward_overrides.get("safety_gate", [])
    for gate in gates:
        if gate["kind"] == "no_allergen_substitute":
            cust = self.data["customers"][self.task.customer_id]
            allergens = set(cust["allergens_hard_flag"])
            for call in self.actions:
                if call.name == "request_substitute":
                    sub = self.data["menus"][(
                        call.kwargs["restaurant_id"],
                        call.kwargs["substitute_item_id"])]
                    if set(sub["allergens"]) & allergens:
                        return False    # hard-zero
    return True

Composed in calculate_reward_shaped() as safety × (outcome + 0.3·process) × monetary. AReaL-SEA validated this style of verifier-based shaping: per-instance verifiers in tau²-bench drove Pass^1 from baseline to 75% retail and 98% telecom. Allergen safety, refund accounting, and policy compliance are responsible-AI primitives built into reward, not bolted on: matches the standard "data privacy, bias mitigation, model monitoring, human-in-the-loop workflows."

Two original moves that compound

1 · Virtual Restaurant Network (VRN)
Persistent two-sided marketplace simulator with shared schema across vertical domains.

50 fictional restaurants, 80 drivers, 200 customers; menus, prep-time profiles, capacity rules. The schema (supplier × courier × consumer × pricing engine) is the abstraction; swap restaurants for clinics, sellers, or technicians and the same generator and simulator produce ride-share, healthcare-scheduling, marketplace, or field-service trajectories.

2 · Difficulty-annotated curriculum splits
The consuming lab trains curriculum-RL out of the box.

Every task tagged: difficulty, multi_step_depth, recovery_required, multi_party. Adds minutes per task; differentiates from flat datasets at near-zero marginal cost.

Reliability is not the same problem as intelligence. the public frame for tau-bench. The data we build is what closes that gap; the playbook makes the next domain cheaper than this one.
